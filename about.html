  • Autobiography

    My full name is Thomas Alexandre Gagné. I was born in London, Brixton, in 2002. Moved to Canada, North Vancouver, when I was only 2 years old. I grew up on the eighth floor of a humble apartment building near Lions gate Bridge. I initially struggled to make friends in school; this became a recurring theme throughout much of my early childhood education. Finding myself using my time to create stories on paper or through other creative means, like LEGO. I struggled with ADHD, often losing focus during class time. It took a lot of trial and error, but eventually I developed methods to focus myself on tasks by making them exciting, separating them into levels, like you would experience in a game, enabling myself to put 100% of my efforts towards a project.

    In 2015, I started my first year of high school. Having moved from the apartment building into a single home, my previous life had mostly disappeared. Classmates I had grown up with, I no longer spoke to. Initially, I was nervous with so many new faces. However, I managed to find the mindset that here, no one knew me. That I could be anyone. I forced myself to be more outgoing, utilizing advice from my father, “It’s normal to be scared or anxious, in the heat of everything, pretend to be confident even if you aren’t”. This line of advice is the single most important reason I am who I am today. By pushing myself to pretend to be someone I am not, hardly ever noticing the change, I soon became that person. No longer needing to pretend.

    In the final years of high school, I put my creative interests on full throttle. Taking internships at printing shops, where I was learning to design for their social media, planning events, and guiding customers. This stoked my passion for design, turning it from a small ember to a heated blaze. Graduating high school in 2020 amidst the chaos of COVID, I started an online course in Graphic Design at VCAD (Visual College of Art and Design). After graduating with my diploma in 2023, I learned two valuable lessons during my education there. That I truly loved design, and that online courses hindered my learning.

    From 2023 to 2025 came my most challenging obstacle, depression. Since losing a majority of my school contacts and spending 2 years indoors, like most, I faced a sense of emptiness. Due to COVID, I felt that I lost that early adulthood life. Additionally, having an online course where communication between students was so minimal, I found myself struggling in social scenes, like I had when I was much younger. Days began to feel like hours, years passing with no notable changes in myself physically or emotionally. As if I were stuck in time, repeating the same actions to no end, or until the end.

    I’m ever grateful to my parents; their support is unmatched. My father offers opportunities to develop skills and push myself. Then my mother, offering her insight, her motivation. I begin working, for the first time since pre-COVID. Finding myself preparing food in a small kitchen on a charter boat. Learning valuable skills like managing pressure, presenting myself/ communicating with customers, and learning to adapt in wildly different work environments like front/ back of house. Then, back at home, I enrolled in the Capilano University IDEA School of Design.

    A life-changing decision for me as I’ve met some of the most amazing people, and had some wonderful experiences in both my school and personal life. My days feel longer and with so much more purpose. I feel renewed, excited for myself and my future. Enthusiastic about my career, taking each step forward with intent and drive, not afraid to learn from past experiences, revisiting regrets and mistakes, ensuring I am happy with myself and that all my decisions build upon my character and sense, deepening my depth as a person.
