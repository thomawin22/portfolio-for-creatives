    Version 3

    My name is Thomas Alexandre Gagné, a UI/UX design student based in North Vancouver with a background in graphic design. I’m driven by curiosity, creativity, and a desire to build experiences that feel intuitive, engaging, and meaningful.

    I didn’t come into design through a straight path. Growing up, I often found it difficult to connect in traditional classroom settings. Instead, I spent my time creating—building stories, designing small worlds, and experimenting with ideas through drawing and LEGO. That instinct to create eventually became the foundation of how I approach design today.

    Living with ADHD forced me to rethink how I focus and work. Over time, I developed systems that turned tasks into structured, engaging challenges—breaking work into “levels” to stay motivated and consistent. What started as a personal coping strategy has become a core part of my design thinking: creating experiences that guide users clearly, reduce friction, and keep them engaged.

    I began my formal design journey studying graphic design at VCAD, where I built a strong foundation in visual communication. During that time, I also worked in hands-on environments—from print shops to customer-facing roles—learning how design exists not just on screens, but in real interactions with people.

    After graduating in 2023, I hit a period of stagnation. Like many, the isolation following COVID impacted both my creativity and sense of direction. But that period ultimately pushed me to make a change.

    Enrolling in the IDEA program at Capilano University became a turning point. Surrounded by a creative community, I rediscovered what I love about design—collaboration, problem-solving, and building things that matter. Since then, I’ve approached every project with renewed energy and intention.

    Today, I’m focused on growing as a UI/UX designer—combining visual design with thoughtful user experience. I’m especially interested in creating systems that feel natural to use, where clarity, structure, and creativity work together seamlessly.
